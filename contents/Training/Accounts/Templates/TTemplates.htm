Templates

What is a template?

Template are a special type of invoice. They have the same functionality as invoice types quote, invoice, receipt, etc. You can place into a template all the billing lines you use for the other invoice types.

Templates are invisible. Customers used in templates are ignored. Prices assigned to templates are not considered in final tots. They never appear in reports or in costings.

When are templates used?

When the button auto-create or apply template is pressed.

Building a template.

Create a new invoice within an account containing the training course that the template will be attached too. Assign the account type as template. (Or copy an existing invoice and re-assign it as template.)

An enrollee name isn't required.

Assign a customer (any customer). The customer selected dictates the discount rate and GST status.

Enter a note .... a describtion of the discount rate and GST status being used. (Enter in a year range is also useful.)

The lock state determines if a template is active (enabled) or not. If the template is locked then it active.

Edit the template and assign the costings.

Nut and bolts - how it work.

When you press auto-create within a new, blank account...

  • a blank invoice is created for every enrollee found in the booking.
  • the customer for that enrollee is assigned to the invoice.
  • based on the course id and the discount rate and GST status (used by the enrollee's customer) every template that matches the criterias is found. 
  • any template that isn't locked is filtered out.
  • the resulting shortlist of templates is ordered by date and the template with the most recent date is used.
  • if something is a-miss ... an error is reported in the notes field.

 

When you press apply templates to an existing invoice...

  • if the invoice contains template items, then they are stripped out of the invoice. Invoice lines manually entered by the booking officier will remain.
  • it then proceeds to match a template based on the criteria rules given above
  • else an error is reported.
